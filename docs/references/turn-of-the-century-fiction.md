Here are summaries of notable fiction published or set between 1890 and 1920 that perfectly capture the occult, gothic horror, and supernatural dread of the era.
## Classic Novels and Novellas

* Dracula by Bram Stoker (1897)
* The Plot: English solicitor Jonathan Harker travels to Transylvania to help a mysterious nobleman buy property in London. Harker soon realizes he is a prisoner in a castle ruled by a vampire. Count Dracula subsequently travels to England, terrorizing the coastal town of Whitby and targeting Harker’s fiancée, Mina. A small group, led by the eccentric Dr. Abraham Van Helsing, must unite to hunt the ancient entity using a mix of modern science and traditional lore.
* The Turn of the Screw by Henry James (1898)
* The Plot: A young, inexperienced governess is hired to care for two orphaned siblings, Miles and Flora, at a remote country estate called Bly. Soon, she begins seeing the silent, spectral figures of the previous governess and a deceased valet wandering the grounds. The story builds an intense psychological dread as the governess becomes convinced that these demonic ghosts are actively possessing and corrupting the children.
* The Beetle by Richard Marsh (1897)
* The Plot: Outselling Dracula when it was first released, this sensation novel follows a nameless, shape-shifting Egyptian entity that infiltrates late-Victorian London. The creature can transform into a monstrous beetle and utilizes mesmeric control and ancient curses to exact revenge on a prominent British politician, exploiting the era's deep-seated anxieties regarding imperial ruin and the occult.
* The Phantom of the Opera by Gaston Leroux (1910)
* The Plot: Eerie accidents and sudden deaths plague the grand Palais Garnier opera house in Paris, all attributed to an elusive "Opera Ghost." The entity is actually Erik, a hideously disfigured musical genius who lives in a hidden, subterranean lair beneath the opera house's dark lake. He becomes utterly obsessed with a young soprano, Christine Daaé, and uses kidnapping, murder, and psychological terror to force her love.

## Cosmic Horror and Occult Detective Works

* The Great God Pan by Arthur Machen (1894)
* The Plot: A scientist performs a radical brain surgery on a young woman to allow her to see the true spiritual reality of the universe, an experience that leaves her catatonic. Years later, a mysterious and beautiful woman named Helen Vaughan arrives in London society. Everywhere Helen goes, wealthy young men are driven to madness and graphic suicide, leading investigators to discover her terrifying, unnatural lineage to an ancient, cosmic deity.
* The Willows by Algernon Blackwood (1907)
* The Plot: Two friends on a canoe trip down the Danube River camp on a remote, sandy island densely covered in weeping willow trees. They are gradually overwhelmed by a heavy, suffocating atmosphere of malice. They realize the island is a thinning point between dimensions, where colossal, unseen forces are shifting the landscape and hunting them from the sky.
* John Silence, Physician Extraordinary by Algernon Blackwood (1908)
* The Plot: A collection of stories following the original "ocult detective." Dr. John Silence is a wealthy, highly trained doctor who uses a combination of advanced psychology, spiritual mediumship, and deep occult knowledge to treat patients afflicted by supernatural curses, ancestral ghosts, and malevolent sorcery.
* The House on the Borderland by William Hope Hodgson (1908)
* The Plot: Two men on a fishing trip in a remote part of Ireland discover the ruins of an ancient stone house built over a massive, dark abyss. Inside, they find the journal of the previous occupant, "The Recluse." The journal details his terrifying experiences defending the home from an onslaught of subterranean swine-monsters, followed by a hallucinatory, cosmic journey through space and time to the literal death of the solar system.
