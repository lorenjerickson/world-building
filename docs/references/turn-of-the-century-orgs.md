To fully understand the global power dynamics of the 1890–1920 era, organizations can be categorized by their massive social, economic, and political influence. This period saw the rise of modern international governance, powerful labor movements, secret societies, and massive corporate monopolies.
## Political and Government Organizations

* The League of Nations (Founded 1920):
* Influence: Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, this was the world's first worldwide intergovernmental organization. It was established after WWI via the Treaty of Versailles to maintain global peace, settle international disputes, and prevent future global conflicts. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] 
* The Black Hand / Crna Ruka (Founded 1911):
* Influence: A secret military society formed by officers in the Kingdom of Serbia. They used state-sponsored terrorism to unite South Slavic territories. Their assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo in 1914 was the direct catalyst that triggered World War I.
* The Bolshevik Party (Founded 1903):
* Influence: Originally a radical faction of the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party led by Vladimir Lenin. They successfully overthrew the Russian Provisional Government during the 1917 October Revolution, pulling Russia out of WWI and laying the foundation for the Soviet Union. [6, 7, 8, 9, 10] 
* The Triple Entente & Triple Alliance (Formed 1907 / 1882):
* Influence: These two massive geopolitical military blocs divided Europe. The Triple Entente (Great Britain, France, Russia) clashed with the Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy), turning a localized Balkan conflict into a global world war. [11, 12, 13] 

## Social, Cultural, and Occult Organizations

* The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn (Active 1888–1903):
* Influence: Based in Great Britain, this secret society was the most influential occult organization of the era. It deeply shaped modern Western esotericism, Tarot, and ritual magic. It included prominent cultural figures like W.B. Yeats, Arthur Machen, and Aleister Crowley.
* The Women’s Social and Political Union / WSPU (Founded 1903):
* Influence: Led by Emmeline Pankhurst in the UK, this militant suffragette organization used high-profile civil disobedience, hunger strikes, and public protests to force the British government to grant women the right to vote, inspiring global suffrage movements. [14, 15] 
* The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People / NAACP (Founded 1909):
* Influence: Founded in the United States by a bi-racial coalition including W.E.B. Du Bois. It became the premier civil rights organization fighting against segregation, Jim Crow laws, and the horrific wave of lynchings sweeping America during this era. [16] 
* The International Committee of the Red Cross / ICRC (Active throughout):
* Influence: This humanitarian organization expanded exponentially during this era. It was instrumental in enforcing the Geneva Conventions, protecting prisoners of war, and providing unprecedented medical relief across the battlefields of the Spanish-American War and WWI.

## Economic and Labor Organizations

* Industrial Workers of the World / IWW (Founded 1905):
* Influence: Known as the "Wobblies," this radical, international labor union aimed to unite all workers into "One Big Union" to overthrow capitalism. They organized massive, highly organized strikes across North America and Europe, facing severe government crackdowns. [17, 18, 19, 20] 
* Standard Oil Trust (Dominant until 1911):
* Influence: Controlled by John D. Rockefeller, this massive corporate empire controlled nearly 90% of oil production and refining in the United States. Its unprecedented economic power led to the landmark 1911 Supreme Court antitrust ruling that forced it to break apart into 34 independent companies. [21, 22, 23, 24] 
* The British South Africa Company / BSAC (Active 1889–1923):
* Influence: A mercantile charter company formed by Cecil Rhodes. It possessed its own private military force and effectively colonized and governed vast territories in southern Africa (Rhodesia) for corporate profit, epitomizing the corporate ruthlessness of New Imperialism.
