To tie this world-building exercise together, here is a gazetteer of highly atmospheric, real-world locations that were central to the history, geopolitics, and gothic anxieties of the 1890–1920 era. These locales serve as perfect anchors for narratives blending industrial progress with cosmic and supernatural dread.
## The Imperial and Industrial Hubs

* London, United Kingdom (The Heart of the Empire)
* Significance: The largest city in the world and the absolute center of global commerce, finance, and imperial power.
   * World-Building Utility: A city of extreme contrasts. It features the grand, electrified estates of high society existing alongside the choking, yellow "pea-souper" fogs of the East End. It serves as the ultimate staging ground for gothic invasions (like Dracula and The Beetle), where ancient horrors infiltrate modern, bureaucratic civilization, and occult groups like the Golden Dawn meet in secret row-house basements.
* Paris, France (The Gilded Shadow)
* Significance: The cultural capital of the Western world, celebrating the height of the Belle Époque and hosting the massive 1900 World’s Fair.
   * World-Building Utility: Underneath the glamorous, gas-lit avenues, art nouveau architecture, and bustling cafes lies a subterranean world. The massive Palais Garnier opera house, with its mythic underground lake, and the sprawling Paris Catacombs pack the city with literal and figurative architectural depth, making it the perfect setting for hidden geniuses, phantoms, and decadent occult societies.
* San Francisco, California, USA (The Ash-Caked Frontier)
* Significance: The premier metropolis of the American West and the primary gateway to the Pacific empire.
   * World-Building Utility: The catastrophic 1906 earthquake and fires reduced Victorian mansions and the bustling Chinatown to apocalyptic ruins. It is a city defined by sudden destruction, rapid rebuilding, and a lawless frontier energy, where the charred silhouettes of the tragedy ("ghost shadows") linger as visual reminders of a sudden, terrifying shift in reality.

## The Frontiers of Cosmic and Sovereign Dread

* The Podkamennaya Tunguska River, Siberia, Russian Empire (The Cosmic Scar)
* Significance: A completely isolated, untamed expanse of subarctic taiga forest.
   * World-Building Utility: Following the unexplained 1908 explosion, this region became a dead zone of skeletal, barkless trees standing upright at an impact site with no crater. Because the Bolshevik Party's subsequent revolution and the collapse of the Russian monarchy locked the country in civil war, the site remained uninvestigated by scientists for decades. It functions as an ideal, unreachable location where something alien, ancient, or apocalyptic tore through the fabric of the Earth.
* Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina (The Powder Keg)
* Significance: A historic Balkan city where the Austro-Hungarian Empire clashed with Slavic nationalism.
   * World-Building Utility: Sarajevo is a crossroads of Islamic, Catholic, and Orthodox architecture, characterized by narrow cobblestone streets and heavy imperial military presence. It is the birthplace of global catastrophe; here, the secret agents of The Black Hand successfully executed the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914, turning a tense, atmospheric city into the literal spark that ignited World War I. [1] 
* Eilean Mòr, Flannan Isles, Scotland (The Lonely Beacon)
* Significance: A remote, storm-battered island in the Outer Hebrides, topped by a newly constructed, state-of-the-art lighthouse.
   * World-Building Utility: The island represents the ultimate isolation of human technology against a vast, uncaring ocean. The unexplained 1900 disappearance of its three keepers—leaving behind a locked lighthouse, a frozen clock, and fabricated or mad log entries—makes it a perfect micro-setting for tales of isolation, madness, or entities rising from the deep Atlantic trench.

## The Zones of Spatial Transformation

* The Panama Canal Zone (The Scar through Space)
* Significance: A strip of land slicing through Central America, engineered by the United States to connect the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
   * World-Building Utility: Opened in 1914, this was an engineering marvel that quite literally altered global geography and naval strategy. The construction was plagued by oppressive tropical heat, dense jungles, and deadly outbreaks of malaria and yellow fever. It serves as a symbol of humanity violently carving up the earth for commerce, inadvertently disturbing ancient, untouched ecosystems.
* No Man’s Land, The Western Front (The Flesh Machine)
* Significance: The scarred, muddy wastes stretching across France and Belgium, separating the Allied and Central trench systems.
   * World-Building Utility: A hellscape of barbed wire, toxic gas clouds, ceaseless artillery fire, and Millions of casualties. This was a place where modern industrial warfare completely shattered the human psyche. It is the ultimate gothic environment, giving rise to mass psychological trauma and battlefield folklore, including the Angels of Mons and rumors of spectral entities or scavenger beasts stalking the dead in the mud.
