The period between 1890 and 1920 was a transformative era defined by the peak of New Imperialism, global industrialization, the devastation of World War I, and massive geopolitical restructuring.
## 1890–1899: Imperial Expansion and Technological Dawn

* Wounded Knee Massacre (1890): U.S. cavalry troops killed nearly 300 Lakota Sioux, marking the definitive end of the American Indian Wars on the standard frontier. [1] 
* First Modern Olympic Games (1896): The ancient athletic tradition was reborn in Athens, Greece, establishing a new platform for peaceful international competition.
* Spanish-American War (1898): This brief conflict collapsed Spain’s remaining empire and established the United States as a global colonial power with the acquisition of Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines. [2] 
* The Boxer Rebellion (1899–1901): An anti-imperialist, anti-Christian uprising in China was crushed by an Eight-Nation Alliance, weakening the Qing dynasty. [3]

* The Wright Brothers' First Flight (1903): Orville and Wilbur Wright achieved the first sustained, controlled flight of a powered aircraft, revolutionizing global transportation and warfare.
* Russo-Japanese War (1904–1905): Japan’s victory over the Russian Empire shocked Western powers, halting Russian expansion in East Asia and validating Japan as a major global force. [2] 
* The Launch of HMS Dreadnought (1906): Great Britain launched a revolutionary battleship that sparked a massive naval arms race, primarily between Britain and Germany. [2, 4] 

## 1910–1920: Global Conflict and Revolution

* The Xinhai Revolution (1911): The overthrow of the Qing dynasty ended over 2,000 years of imperial rule in China and led to the establishment of the Republic of China.
* Sinking of the RMS Titanic (1912): The "unsinkable" luxury ocean liner sank on its maiden voyage after hitting an iceberg, resulting in over 1,500 deaths and triggering global maritime safety reforms.
* Opening of the Panama Canal (1914): The engineering marvel connected the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, fundamentally altering international trade routes and naval strategy.
* World War I (1914–1918): Triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, the "Great War" pitted the Allied Powers against the Central Powers. It caused 20 million deaths, collapsed the German, Austro-Hungarian, Russian, and Ottoman empires, and reshaped the global map.
* The Russian Revolution (1917): The collapse of the Russian monarchy gave rise to the Bolsheviks under Vladimir Lenin. This led directly to the creation of the Soviet Union, the world's first communist state.
* The 1918 Influenza Pandemic (1918–1920): Often called the "Spanish Flu," this global pandemic infected one-third of the world's population and killed an estimated 17 million to 50 million people, eclipsing the mortality rate of WWI.
* The Treaty of Versailles and the League of Nations (1919–1920): The peace treaty officially ended WWI and established the [League of Nations](https://history.state.gov/milestones/1914-1920/league), the first intergovernmental organization aimed at maintaining world peace.
